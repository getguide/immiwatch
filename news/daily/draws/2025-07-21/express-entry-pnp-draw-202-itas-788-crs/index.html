On July 21, 2025, Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) conducted an Express Entry draw specifically for Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) candidates. A total of 202 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) were issued to candidates who had a minimum Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score of 788.

Key Highlights

This draw reflects IRCC's ongoing strategy of holding program-specific selections, targeting candidates with provincial support. The high CRS cutoff of 788 indicates the competitive nature of PNP candidates, who typically have enhanced profiles due to their provincial nominations.

üéØ Strategic Focus

This PNP-only draw continues IRCC's trend of program-specific selections, focusing on provincial nominees who typically have enhanced profiles and provincial support. The targeted approach allows IRCC to address specific labor market needs across different provinces.

Analysis of the Draw

CRS Score Analysis

The CRS cutoff of 788 is significantly higher than general draws, which is typical for PNP-specific rounds. This reflects the fact that PNP candidates receive 600 additional CRS points for their provincial nomination, making them highly competitive in the Express Entry pool.

Program-Specific Strategy

IRCC's approach of conducting program-specific draws allows for more targeted immigration selection. PNP-only draws ensure that provinces can effectively address their specific labor market needs while maintaining the integrity of the Express Entry system.

Impact on Candidates

For PNP candidates, this draw represents an opportunity to receive an ITA with their provincial nomination. The 202 ITAs issued will help address specific labor market needs across participating provinces.

For general Express Entry candidates without provincial nominations, this draw does not affect their chances, as it was specifically targeted at PNP candidates.

Looking Ahead

IRCC is expected to continue its mixed approach of general draws and program-specific selections throughout 2025. This strategy allows for more responsive immigration planning that addresses both national and provincial priorities.
